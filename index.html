Chevron Refinery

And its effects on real estate


The Chevron Richmond Refinery is a petroleum refinery in Richmond, California that is owned and operated by the Chevron Coorporation. It is the city's largest employer and taxpayer, providing over 1,200 jobs. Chevron, and the refinery, have played a very important and dramactic role in the Richmond community, affecting housing prices, economic diversity, and the environment around Richmond.


The Effects of the Chevron Richmond Refinery on the Environment

This image shows the air quality in the Bay Area. Purple signifies worse air quality and green signifies better air quality. Around the Richmond area, you can see that the air quality is significantly worse than many other parts of the Bay.

High polution can cause disease in children. Roughly 30 out of every 10,000 children in Richmond require hospitalization for the respiratory illness, according to a 2011 Kaiser Family Foundation study.

Around 762 pounds of h2s (hydrogen sulfide) is released, along with around 382.8 pounds of Carbon Monoxide.

The Effect on Richmond Due to Housing Prices

The richmond refinery also apears to affect schools. As it turns out, the close you are to the refinery the lower the quality of education becomes.
